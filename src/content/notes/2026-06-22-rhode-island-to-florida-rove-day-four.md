Day four of the [Rhode Island-to-Florida POTA
rove](/projects/2026-06-rove-to-fl/) was a travel day from Fredericksburg,
Virginia toward Florence, South Carolina. The radio plan was modest after the
big DC push the day before: activate [Fredericksburg and Spotsylvania National
Military Park, US-0717](/radio/pota/US-0717/) before leaving Virginia, then stop
at [Medoc Mountain State Park, US-2744](/radio/pota/US-2744/) in North Carolina
on the way south.

I finished with 23 CW QSOs across the two parks: 10 at Fredericksburg and
Spotsylvania and 13 at Medoc Mountain. The main lesson came before the first
contact. At a historic site with tours, visitors, and staff trying to manage the
grounds, it is worth checking in with a ranger before unpacking even when the
parking lot looks empty.

## At a glance

- **Where:** [Fredericksburg and Spotsylvania National Military Park, US-0717](/radio/pota/US-0717/) in Virginia and [Medoc Mountain State Park, US-2744](/radio/pota/US-2744/) in North Carolina
- **When:** June 22, 2026 local date; roughly 1436-1501 UTC at Fredericksburg and 1952-2025 UTC at Medoc Mountain
- **Activation:** 23 CW QSOs total; three on 40 meters and 20 on 20 meters
- **Radio:** Yaesu FT-891
- **Antenna:** PackTenna 29-foot random wire at both stops
- **CW gear:** N3ZN paddle, with the leg strap used after a short handheld attempt

## [Fredericksburg and Spotsylvania National Military Park, US-0717](/radio/pota/US-0717/)

I left the hotel while Max was still sleeping and aimed for a quick morning
activation before the drive south. I stopped for coffee, went to the visitor
center, and found what looked like a good open field near the parking lot. The
lot was empty, nothing obvious was happening yet, and I figured it would be a
straightforward place to put up the 25-foot whip in a Rybakov-style setup.

That was the wrong order of operations. I had the station set up and was getting
ready to get on the air when a ranger came out and asked me to pack it up and
move along. He was kind about it and not upset, but that field was part of the
visitor-center tour flow. The tours were starting a little later, and my setup
would have been in the way or at least created a distraction.

I completely understood what he was trying to protect. I also should have asked
first instead of waiting until there was a problem. With the false start, I had
to tear everything back down, reload the car, and find another operating spot
while the morning schedule slipped.

The second spot worked out well. I drove to Lee's Hill, which was historically
called Telegraph Hill. That felt like a fun place to make radio contacts, and
it kept me inside the same park reference without using the visitor-center
field. This time I kept the activation simple: FT-891 in the car, PackTenna
29-foot random wire, and the N3ZN paddle.

I tried holding the N3ZN paddle by hand for a minute, but that made it too easy
to hit the wrong side of the key with the hand that was holding it. The leg
strap was much better and gave me a steady in-car CW position.

Once I actually turned the radio on, the activation was quick. I made 10 CW QSOs
from 1436 to 1501 UTC: three on 40 meters, then seven on 20 meters. The whole
working part of the stop was about 25 minutes, with roughly five minutes to set
up and five minutes to tear down after the visitor-center reset. [VO1AW](https://www.qrz.com/db/VO1AW)
found me again from Newfoundland, and [KG1A](https://www.qrz.com/db/KG1A) gave
me a park-to-park from US-6305.

## [Medoc Mountain State Park, US-2744](/radio/pota/US-2744/)

After Fredericksburg I went back to the hotel, got Max coffee, packed up, and
we pointed the car toward Florence. The North Carolina stop was Medoc Mountain
State Park, and it was a quiet, pleasant park and a straightforward car
activation.

I used the FT-891, the same 29-foot end-fed random wire, and the N3ZN paddle
again.

I made 13 CW QSOs on 20 meters from 1952 to 2025 UTC. The first contact was
[WC1D](https://www.qrz.com/db/WC1D) as a park-to-park from US-4953, and my
friend Brian, [K1NW](https://www.qrz.com/db/K1NW), also made it into the log
from Rhode Island, which was a nice full-circle contact from North Carolina.

## What worked

- The Lee's Hill pivot saved the Fredericksburg activation without turning the
  morning into a long stop.
- The PackTenna random wire and in-car FT-891 setup stayed quick once I stopped
  trying to use the visitor-center field.
- The N3ZN paddle on the leg strap was the right CW position in the car.
- Medoc Mountain was a simple enough stop to fit into a long driving day.

## To adjust next time

- At National Park Service sites with visitor-center areas, ask a ranger before
  setting up in a visible field or tour corridor.
- Keep the leg strap with the in-car CW gear instead of trying to handhold the
  paddle first.
- Write down the antenna and operating position for every stop, even when the
  activation feels routine.
